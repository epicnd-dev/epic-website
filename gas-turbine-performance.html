Work with GE Power to create a model of gas turbine performance that predicts its energy yield, carbon emissions, and nitrogen oxide emissions. Members of the team will use data science, machine learning, and knowledge of thermodynamics to build and optimize their model.

Project goals include understanding how a combined cycle power plant works, developing models to predict energy output and harmful emissions, and determining if it is possible for such power plants to be carbon neutral.

The project begins early March and lasts through spring semester for a duration of approximately 10-12 weeks. The first one or two weeks will serve as an introduction to gas turbines and how combined cycle power plants operate. Thereafter, the team will work on building its model through combined effort and knowledge from its members’ majors and meet with GE for guidance and evaluation throughout the semester. We also aim to provide opportunities for the team to give presentations and write small technical memorandums, culminating in a final presentation at the end of the semester; this will allow students to build both technical writing and presentation skills on top of the engineering skills they will acquire from the creation of the model. Members are asked to commit 3-5 hours per week as they work with an interdisciplinary team of engineers.

Engineering Projects in Industry Club

Notre Dame

Mission

We aim to enrich the Notre Dame engineering curriculum by providing an avenue for students to gain industry experience and apply theory in class to real-world projects.

About

epic is new club at notre dame that organizes project teams comprised of students interested in solving engineering problems with industry leaders. through such interdisciplinary projects, students develop the technical and communication skills that are utilized everyday by those working in industry. Each project will follow a similar cycle as a real industry project, including the Engineering design cycle, prototyping, analysis, code development, etc. we hope students are able to build relationships with partnering companies that can evolve into internship or employment opportunities.
